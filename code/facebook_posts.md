# Facebook Group Strategy — Resume Reality Check

**Goal:** 3 free audits this week → testimonials → 1-2 paid audits next week

---

## Step 1: Groups to Join (in order of priority)

| Group | Why | Action |
|-------|-----|--------|
| "Free Resume Review" | Literally built for this | Join immediately |
| "Job Search Support Group" | High-intent job seekers | Join + check rules |
| "Career Coaching & Job Search Tips" | Professionals seeking help | Join + check rules |
| "I Want A Good Job" | Active, general job search | Join + check rules |
| Any local "Job Seekers [City]" group | Smaller = more personal | Optional |

**Before posting in any group:** Read the pinned rules. Look for language about:
- "No self-promotion" → ask admin before posting
- "Services allowed with disclosure" → post freely
- "Weekly promo threads" → wait for those threads
- Nothing about it → post but stay value-first

---

## Step 2: First 48 Hours — Build Trust Before Offering Anything

Find existing posts where people are asking for resume feedback or venting about job search.

**Comment like this:**
```
Your summary is doing more harm than good — "motivated team player seeking opportunities"
tells recruiters nothing. Try leading with your actual title + one credible claim.

Something like: "Marketing Manager with 5 years in B2B SaaS. Led campaigns that generated
$2M in pipeline in 2024."

That's the recruiter's 6-second scan — give them the answer immediately.
```

Be specific. Use their actual words if possible. Don't mention paid service yet.
Do this on 5-10 posts before making any offer.

---

## Step 3: The Offer Post (Day 3-4)

**Post this after you've commented helpfully on several posts:**

```
I've been reviewing resumes as a side project and want to be useful. Offering 3 free,
written resume audits this week.

What you get: a specific breakdown of what's working, what's not, and the top 3 things
to fix — based on your actual resume, not generic advice.

Not a rewrite. Not a sales pitch for coaching. Just honest, specific feedback.

If you want one: comment your industry + target role below. I'll pick 3 and DM you.

(I'll eventually charge for this, but want to test the format first and get real feedback.)
```

---

## Step 4: When Someone Responds

Reply: "Perfect — I'll DM you."

DM them:
```
Thanks for reaching out! To get started, can you:

1. Paste your resume text in this DM (or attach as a PDF)
2. Tell me your target role / job title
3. Your industry and level (entry / mid / senior)
4. Optional: paste a job description you're actively targeting

I'll send your audit back within 24-48 hours.
```

---

## Step 5: Delivering the Audit

Use the framework in `audit_framework.md` to structure the feedback.

The format:
```
--- RESUME REALITY CHECK ---
[Name] | [Role Target] | [Date]

OVERALL TAKE:
[2-3 sentences. What's working. What's the #1 problem.]

...
[Full audit per the framework]
...

TOP 3 PRIORITIES:
1. [Most important fix]
2. [Second fix]
3. [Third fix]
---
```

---

## Step 6: After the Free Audit — The Ask

Send this 24-48 hours after they receive the audit:

```
Hope that was useful — happy to answer any follow-up questions.

One thing I'd love to know: if this had been a paid service, what would it have been
worth to you? Even a rough ballpark. I'm figuring out pricing before I launch properly.

And if you found it helpful, a quick sentence about your experience would mean a lot —
I'm collecting feedback before I go public.
```

**Two asks:** Price they'd pay + a testimonial sentence.
Both are data. Both are valuable.

---

## Step 7: Converting to Paid

After 3 free audits, post again:

```
Wrapped up the free resume audits I offered last week. Got some good feedback that
helped me refine the format.

Going to start charging for these — $12 for a full written audit. Same thing:
specific notes on your actual resume, not generic advice. 48-hour turnaround.

If you're interested: [Ko-Fi link: https://ko-fi.com/s/ad6bf4c892]

Happy to answer questions before you buy — just comment or DM.
```

## Metrics to Track

- Groups joined: target 5 this week
- Comments on existing posts: target 10 before making an offer
- Free audit offer responses: target 3-5
- Free audits delivered: target 3
- "Would you have paid?" responses: collect all of them
- Testimonials collected: target 3
- Paid audits week 2: target 1-2

---

## Fallback: If Facebook Groups Don't Work

If zero response to free offer after 48 hours:
1. Try a different group (or more direct message in a smaller group)
2. Try Discord — specifically "The Assist" or tech career servers
3. Ask Aarin to share the Ko-Fi link on her personal Twitter/X or LinkedIn story (low effort, tests warm network)

---

## What This Proves / Disproves

✅ PROVEN if: 3+ people respond to the free offer → problem is real, people are willing to engage
✅ PROVEN if: After receiving audit, someone says "I'd pay $10-15 for this" → pricing confirmed
❌ DISPROVEN if: Zero responses → problem isn't urgent, or wrong audience, or wrong positioning
❌ DISPROVEN if: They respond but don't submit their resume → friction is too high, simplify the intake
